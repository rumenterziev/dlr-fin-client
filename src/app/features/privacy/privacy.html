This Privacy Policy explains what personal data Rumen.dev ("we", "us") collects when you use this site and its applications, why we collect it, and what your rights are under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

1. Who we are

This site is operated by Rumen Terziev, an individual developer. Contact for privacy requests: see the link in the footer.

2. What we collect

  • Account data (if you sign in with Google): your name, email address and Google profile picture URL. We do not receive or store your Google password.
  • Session cookie: a single strictly-necessary cookie used to keep you signed in. No advertising or third-party tracking cookies are used.
  • Conversion history (signed-in users only): the inputs and results of currency conversions you run while signed in, so you can review them later.
  • Chat messages (signed-in users only): messages you send to the AI assistant are forwarded to an AI provider to generate a response.
  • Consent record: when you accept this policy, we store the policy version and timestamp in your browser's localStorage.

3. What we do not collect

  • No advertising or analytics tracking cookies.
  • No fingerprinting, cross-site tracking or third-party advertising pixels.
  • Anonymous use of the currency converter is not persisted to your account.

4. Legal basis

We rely on the following GDPR lawful bases:

  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): when you tick "I agree" before signing in with Google.
  • Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)): to provide the signed-in features you request (e.g. saving your conversion history).
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): to keep the service secure (e.g. the session cookie).

5. Sharing

We share the minimum data required to operate the service: messages you send to the AI assistant are processed by our AI provider. We do not sell your personal data.

6. Retention

  • Account data: kept until you delete your account.
  • Conversion history: kept until you delete it or your account.
  • Session cookie: expires when your session ends.

7. Your rights

Under GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correct inaccurate data.
  • Delete your account and associated data ("right to be forgotten").
  • Export your data in a portable format.
  • Withdraw consent at any time.
  • Lodge a complaint with your local data-protection authority.

8. Children

This service is not directed at children under 16 and we do not knowingly collect their data.

9. Changes

When this policy changes materially we bump the version number above and ask you to re-accept on next sign-in.
